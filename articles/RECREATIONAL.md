### Chemical-Free Swimming in Harmony with Nature

**The Natural Swimming Pool Revolution**

Inspired by mountain lakes and natural swimming holes, natural pools (also called swim ponds or eco-pools) combine the refreshment of swimming with the beauty of natural water featuresâ€”without chemicals.

---

### What is a Natural Swimming Pool?

**Definition:**
A natural swimming pool is a man-made body of water that uses biological filtration instead of chemicals to maintain clean, swimmable water.

**Key Components:**

**1. Swimming Zone:**
- Clear water area for swimming
- Typically 2-4 meters deep
- Smooth, swimmable bottom
- No plant growth in swimming area

**2. Regeneration Zone:**
- Planted area with aquatic plants
- Acts as biological filter
- 30-50% of total surface area
- Shallow (30-80cm depth)

**3. Filtration System:**
- Natural (plants, beneficial bacteria, substrate)
- Optional mechanical (skimmer, pump)
- Optional biological (biofilm reactors)
- Circulation to maintain water quality

---

### How Natural Pools Work

**Biological Filtration Process:**

**Stage 1: Mechanical Filtration**
- Debris removed by skimmer
- Solids settled in pre-filter
- Coarse materials trapped

**Stage 2: Biological Filtration**
- Water flows through regeneration zone
- Bacteria colonize gravel and roots
- Nutrients absorbed by plants
- Algae outcompeted by aquatic plants

**Stage 3: Plant Uptake**
- Aquatic plants absorb nitrates and phosphates
- Oxygenate water through photosynthesis
- Provide habitat for beneficial organisms
- Create natural balance

**Stage 4: Circulation**
- Gentle water movement
- Prevents stagnation
- Distributes oxygen
- Maintains clarity

**Result:** Crystal-clear, chemical-free water safe for swimming

### What Makes a Proper Koi Pond?

**Not Just a Pond with Fish:**

A true koi pond is an engineered aquatic life support system designed specifically for koi carp (Cyprinus carpio), one of the most demanding ornamental fish species.

**Key Requirements:**

**1. Size & Volume:**
- Minimum: 1,000 gallons (3,800 liters)
- Recommended: 2,000+ gallons (7,500+ liters)
- Rule of thumb: 250-300 gallons per koi
- Depth: Minimum 3 feet (0.9m), ideal 4-6 feet (1.2-1.8m)

**2. Filtration:**
- Mechanical filtration (remove solids)
- Biological filtration (process ammonia/nitrite)
- 3-5 times pond volume per hour circulation
- Filter capacity: 20-30% of pond volume

**3. Water Quality:**
- pH: 7.0-8.5
- Ammonia: 0 ppm
- Nitrite: 0 ppm
- Nitrate: <40 ppm
- Dissolved Oxygen: >6 ppm
- Temperature: 59-77Â°F (15-25Â°C) optimal

**4. Aeration:**
- Multiple air stones or diffusers
- Waterfalls for oxygen transfer
- Adequate surface agitation

**5. Bottom Drain:**
- Critical for solid waste removal
- Centrally located
- Gravity-fed to filter
- Prevents sludge accumulation
