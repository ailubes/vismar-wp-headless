### Phase 1: Project Definition & Objectives

**Objective:** Establish clear scope, goals, and success criteria

**Our Process:**
1. **Stakeholder Workshops**
   - Identify all key stakeholders
   - Understand investment goals (ROI targets, timeline, risk tolerance)
   - Define operational objectives (production volume, species, markets)

2. **Preliminary Concept Development**
   - Production system alternatives (RAS, HFTS, ponds, cages)
   - Target species options
   - Scale and phasing strategies

3. **Success Criteria Definition**
   - Financial metrics (IRR, NPV, payback period)
   - Production targets (tons/year, survival rates, FCR)
   - Sustainability goals (water use, energy efficiency, waste management)

**Deliverables:**
- Project objectives document
- Preliminary concept options (3-5 alternatives)
- Evaluation criteria matrix

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

### Phase 2: Site Assessment & Analysis

**Objective:** Evaluate site suitability for proposed aquaculture operation

**Our Process:**

#### 2.1 Site Visit & Physical Assessment
- **Location Analysis**
  - GPS coordinates and mapping
  - Topography and land characteristics
  - Soil quality and bearing capacity
  - Drainage and flood risk
  - Access roads and logistics

- **Infrastructure Assessment**
  - Existing buildings and utilities
  - Power availability and capacity
  - Water supply sources
  - Wastewater disposal options
  - Communication infrastructure

#### 2.2 Water Source Analysis
- **Quantity Assessment**
  - Available volume (wells, surface water, municipal)
  - Flow rates and seasonal variations
  - Reliability and backup sources
  - Water rights and allocation

- **Quality Testing**
  - Physical parameters (temperature, turbidity)
  - Chemical analysis (pH, alkalinity, hardness, dissolved oxygen)
  - Biological parameters (pathogens, algae)
  - Heavy metals and contaminants
  - Suitability for target species

#### 2.3 Climate & Environmental Conditions
- Temperature ranges (air and water)
- Precipitation patterns
- Solar radiation (for solar energy potential)
- Wind conditions
- Seismic activity
- Natural disaster risks

#### 2.4 Regulatory & Legal Assessment
- **Zoning & Permits**
  - Land use regulations
  - Environmental permits required
  - Water extraction licenses
  - Building permits
  - Aquaculture operation licenses

- **Compliance Requirements**
  - Environmental impact assessment needs
  - Food safety regulations
  - Labor law compliance
  - Tax implications

- **Stakeholder Mapping**
  - Community relations
  - Environmental groups
  - Competing water users
  - Local authorities

**Deliverables:**
- Site assessment report with maps and photos
- Water quality analysis results
- Regulatory requirements checklist
- Site suitability rating (1-10 scale)
- Potential deal-breakers identified

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

### Phase 3: Technical Feasibility

**Objective:** Determine optimal production system and validate technical viability

**Our Process:**

#### 3.1 Species Selection Analysis
- **Biological Suitability**
  - Temperature tolerance vs. local climate
  - Water quality requirements vs. available sources
  - Growth rates and production cycles
  - Disease resistance and biosecurity

- **Economic Considerations**
  - Market prices and stability
  - Feed costs and availability
  - Seed/fingerling costs and sources
  - Labor requirements

- **Technology Match**
  - System complexity vs. operator experience
  - Local expertise availability
  - Technology maturity and proven track record

**Species We Commonly Evaluate:**
- **Shrimp:** Vannamei (L. vannamei), Black Tiger (P. monodon)

**Objective:** Create detailed financial projections to evaluate investment viability

**Our Process:**

#### 5.1 Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) Estimation

**Land & Site Development:**
- Land purchase or lease
- Site preparation (grading, drainage)
- Access roads and parking
- Utilities connection (power, water, internet)
- Landscaping and fencing

**Buildings & Structures:**
- Production buildings
- Processing facility
- Cold storage
- Office and staff facilities
- Maintenance workshop
- Storage buildings (feed, equipment)

**Production Equipment:**
- Fish tanks and raceways
- Water treatment systems
- Aeration and oxygenation
- Temperature control equipment
- Pumps and plumbing
- Backup generators
- Monitoring and control systems

**Supporting Equipment:**
- Feed storage and dispensing
- Harvesting equipment
- Grading and sorting systems
- Processing equipment
- Refrigeration units
- Vehicles and forklifts

**Professional Services:**
- Feasibility study
- Detailed engineering design
- Environmental impact assessment
- Permitting and legal fees
- Project management

**Contingency:**
- 10-20% of total CAPEX for unforeseen costs

**Total CAPEX Breakdown:**
```
Typical Distribution (varies by project):
- Land & Site: 10-20%
- Buildings: 20-30%
- Production Equipment: 30-40%
- Supporting Infrastructure: 10-15%
- Professional Services: 5-10%
- Contingency: 10-15%
```

#### 5.2 Operating Expenditure (OPEX) Estimation

**Variable Costs (per ton produced):**
- **Feed:** 45-60% of operating costs
  - Feed price per ton
  - Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)
  - Total feed required annually

- **Seed/Fingerlings:** 10-20%
  - Price per unit
  - Stocking density
  - Survival rate assumptions

- **Labor (Production):** 5-10%
  - Number of workers
  - Wages and benefits
  - Productivity assumptions

- **Energy:** 10-20%
  - Electricity consumption (kWh/ton produced)
  - Electricity rates
  - Fuel for generators/heating

- **Other Variable Costs:** 5-10%
  - Chemicals and treatments
  - Probiotics and supplements
  - Packaging materials
  - Transport and logistics

**Fixed Costs (annual):**
- **Labor (Management & Admin):**
  - Manager/supervisor salaries
  - Administrative staff
  - Technical specialists

- **Utilities (Fixed Charges):**
  - Base utility fees
  - Internet and communications

- **Maintenance & Repairs:**
  - 3-5% of equipment value annually
  - Preventive maintenance

- **Insurance:**
  - Property insurance
  - Liability insurance
  - Crop insurance (if available)

- **Regulatory & Compliance:**
  - License renewals
  - Water quality testing
  - Health inspections
  - Certifications

- **Marketing & Sales:**
  - Marketing materials
  - Sales commissions
  - Trade shows and events

- **General & Administrative:**
  - Office supplies
  - Professional services
  - Travel
  - Training and development

#### 5.3 Financial Projections (10-Year Model)

**Revenue Model:**
```
Year 1: Ramp-up (30-50% capacity)
Year 2: Growing (60-80% capacity)
Year 3: Target capacity (80-100%)
Year 4-10: Steady state with growth
```

**Key Financial Statements:**
1. **Profit & Loss (P&L) Statement**
   - Revenue by product line
   - Cost of goods sold (COGS)
   - Gross profit and margin
   - Operating expenses
   - EBITDA
   - Depreciation and amortization
   - Interest expense
   - Taxes
   - Net income

2. **Cash Flow Statement**
   - Operating cash flow
   - Investment cash flow
   - Financing cash flow
   - Free cash flow
   - Cumulative cash position

3. **Balance Sheet**
   - Assets (current and fixed)
   - Liabilities (current and long-term)
   - Equity
   - Working capital

#### 5.4 Investment Analysis

**Financial Metrics:**
- **Net Present Value (NPV)**
  - Discount rate: 8-12% (typical)
  - NPV > 0 indicates viable project

- **Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
  - Target: >15% for aquaculture projects
  - Compare to investor's hurdle rate

- **Payback Period**
  - Time to recover initial investment
  - Simple payback: 5-7 years typical
  - Discounted payback: 6-9 years typical

- **Return on Investment (ROI)**
  - Average annual return
  - Comparison to alternative investments

- **Profitability Index**
  - NPV / Initial Investment
  - PI > 1.0 indicates value creation

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- Production volume break-even
- Revenue break-even
- Sensitivity to price and cost changes

#### 5.5 Financing Structure

**Equity vs. Debt:**
- Recommended debt-to-equity ratio: 50/50 to 70/30
- Equity: Owner contribution, investors
- Debt: Bank loans, development finance, grants

**Debt Service:**
- Loan amount
- Interest rate
- Loan term
- Repayment schedule
- Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR): Target >1.25

**Funding Sources:**
- Owner's equity
- Angel investors / VC
- Commercial bank loans
- Government grants/subsidies
- Development finance institutions
- Crowdfunding

#### 5.6 Sensitivity Analysis

**Key Variables to Test:**
1. **Selling Price** (¬±10%, ¬±20%)
2. **Production Yield** (¬±10%, ¬±20%)
3. **Feed Costs** (¬±10%, ¬±20%)
4. **CAPEX Overrun** (+10%, +20%, +30%)
5. **Ramp-up Delay** (6 months, 12 months)

**Scenario Analysis:**
- **Base Case:** Most likely scenario
- **Best Case:** Optimistic assumptions
- **Worst Case:** Conservative assumptions
- **Stress Test:** Multiple worst-case factors combined

**Output:**
- Tornado chart showing most impactful variables
- Scenario comparison table
- Monte Carlo simulation (if requested)

**Deliverables:**
- Detailed financial model (Excel)
- CAPEX breakdown by category
- OPEX breakdown by category

**Example Mitigation Plans:**

- Contingency: Emergency response plan, veterinary support

- Impact reduction: Maintain lower stocking densities

- Mitigation: Long-term supply contracts, market diversification
- Contingency: Reduce production, store product
- Transfer: Forward contracts, hedging (if available)
- Impact reduction: Multiple species/products

**Equipment Failure Risk:**
- Mitigation: Redundant systems, preventive maintenance
- Contingency: Spare parts inventory, emergency suppliers
- Transfer: Equipment warranties, service contracts
- Impact reduction: Backup power, multiple production units
